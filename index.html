Introduction

Goat tracking, the practice of monitoring and recording the movements, behaviors, and health of goats, has gained importance in modern livestock management. Traditionally, goats have been kept in small herds, often in remote or rugged areas, where monitoring them manually can be challenging. However, advances in technology have introduced innovative ways to track goats, such as GPS devices, collars, and smart sensors. These tools provide farmers, herders, and researchers with critical insights into the animals' habits, health, and the environmental conditions in which they thrive. This essay explores the significance of goat tracking, its benefits, methods, and its role in improving goat farming and sustainable livestock management.
